Distribution of wealth in MEXICO
Mexico is one of the countries with the most social inequality in America Latina Only 10% of families accumulate two thirds of the national wealth and 1% of those families has more than a third of Mexican wealth, according to the Social Panorama report of Latin America 2016, of the Economic Commission for America Latin America and the Caribbean (ECLAC). Mexican women are the most used of their time to activities for which they do not receive economic remuneration. And by Unpaid work refers to care and domestic chores. While men only occupy 11.6% of their time.

In Mexico 210 thousand people who have investments in the Bolsa Mexicana de Valores (BMV) have almost a fifth part (22 percent) of the national wealth, says a report of the Economic Commission for Latin America and the Caribbean (Cepal). According to the study The distribution and inequality of financial and non-financial assets in Mexico, prepared by Miguel del Castillo Negrete, the agency said that the country ranks 20th in the classification of nations with more millionaires (according to a list of Credit Suisse), but at the same time it is located on site 15 in the list of those who have the greatest number of people who They do not have adequate access to food. The report points out that despite being a country with a lot of natural wealth (as it has minerals and oil, an extensive coast, water resources, forests and sufficient surface agricultural sector to feed its inhabitants) the problem of Mexico is that unfortunately the distribution is very unequal.
